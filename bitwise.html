Bit Fields Explained (or, What Does pte & PTE_P Do?)

Numbers in computers are represented in binary. And binary numbers are made up of binary digits, or bits per short. A bit can either be a 1 or a 0.

Operations can be applied to the individual bits of numbers. These operations are called bitwise operators.

~

The ~, or Bitwise NOT operator, takes all the 0s in a number and makes them 1s; and takes all the 1s and makes them 0s. For instance, consider the number 1234, which is 00000000000000000000010011010010 as a 32-bit integer in binary.
